## Overview

On May 29, 2025, Anthropic released an open-source circuit tracing toolkit that redefines how researchers interpret large language models. At the core of this release is the **attribution graph**—an interactive, visual representation of how neural networks reason and generate output.

This groundbreaking development marks a pivotal step toward **AI transparency**, offering an actionable roadmap for understanding model internals and decision pathways in real time.

## Why It Matters

Modern AI systems exhibit capabilities far beyond our interpretability frameworks. This toolkit directly addresses that gap by enabling researchers to **see inside** language models and explore the exact computations that shape their outputs.

> "The future of AI safety and trust depends on our ability to explain how models think. This release is a catalyst for that understanding."
> — *Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic*

## Core Features

- **Attribution Graphs**: Map and visualize the internal circuits a model uses to process inputs and generate outputs.
- **Interactive Graph UI**: Navigate and explore neuron pathways using an intuitive Neuronpedia interface.
- **Circuit Tracing**: Automatically generate detailed reasoning graphs for supported transformer models.
- **Live Hypothesis Testing**: Modify feature values and observe real-time behavioral changes.
- **Cross-Model Compatibility**: Includes support for open models such as Gemma-2–2b and Llama-3.2–1b.

## The Neuronpedia Experience

The **Neuronpedia frontend** transforms complex graphs into interactive visuals, making it easier for the broader research community to access and manipulate attribution data without deep technical setups.

> Visit: [https://neuronpedia.org](https://neuronpedia.org)

## Use Cases

### 1. Advanced Circuit Analysis
Generate and study model-specific reasoning flows to analyze high-level decision patterns.

### 2. Collaborative Interpretability
Annotate graphs, share findings, and crowdsource circuit identification with fellow researchers.

### 3. Experimental Hypothesis Testing
Modify internal representations or inputs and evaluate causal relationships between neural activity and model behavior.

## Early Insights

- **Multi-Step Reasoning**: Reveal how models break down logical tasks.
- **Language Representation**: Visualize translation pathways and multilingual node structures.
- **Cross-Model Analysis**: Compare how different architectures approach similar tasks.
